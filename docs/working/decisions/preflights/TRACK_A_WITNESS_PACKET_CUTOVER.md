## One-line claim

> Track A claim evaluators should consume witness packets, not promote findings into coverage.

That sentence is the whole job. Everything below is the discipline required to honor it without quietly rebuilding the old shape inside the new vocabulary.

## Keeper

> **Witnesses observe; evaluators preflight; receipts bind custody.**

Meaner restatement, for use when somebody proposes a shortcut:

> A finding may report a conclusion. It may not become the witness that authorized itself.

Compact restatement for the receipt-side discipline:

> A receipt should be able to tell whether it binds observation, projection, or vibes.

Less cute version of the same line:

> Receipts must distinguish native witness custody from transitional compatibility custody.

## The custody gap this slice closes

Track A today builds claim preflight coverage from detector / finding state. That works, but it leaves a custody gap that Slices 1a–1e (Phase 2 of `PATH_TO_1_0.md`) made operationally visible:

- Findings can describe evidence.
- Findings can summarize detector conclusions.
- Findings can feed evaluators.
- **Findings are not themselves witness packets.**

This means Track A receipts cannot cleanly name the observation artifact whose custody they depend on. `nq-monitor receipt replay` correctly returns `REPLAY_NOT_APPLICABLE` for every Track A receipt today (per `RECEIPT_REPLAY.md`), and that bounded-honest answer is what motivates this cut-over.

## Three pipeline shapes

The desired flow:

```text
substrate / collector observation
  → witness packet (nq.witness.v1)
  → claim preflight evaluator
  → verdict
  → receipt that names the witness packet digest
```

Not the current flow:

```text
substrate / collector observation
  → finding
  → inferred coverage
  → verdict
  → receipt that cites no retained packet envelope
```

And under no circumstances:

```text
finding
  → witness packet reconstructed-after-the-fact-but-presented-as-native
  → receipt
```

The third shape is the failure mode this design preflight exists to prevent. Reconstructing a witness packet from finding state may be necessary as a transitional measure; pretending the reconstructed packet has the same custody standing as a native one is exactly the laundering pattern NQ exists to refuse.

## Invariants

These are the non-negotiables. Any implementation that violates one of them violates the whole point of the cut-over.

### 1. Witnesses observe; they do not promote

A witness packet may testify about observed substrate state. It may not decide what operational claim follows from that state.

Allowed (a packet may say):

- mount `/var` observed at 91% byte usage
- inode usage observed at 72%
- observation generated by `fs_usage` witness profile v0
- packet has no testimony for recovery, app health, or future exhaustion

Not allowed (a packet may not say):

- disk is healthy
- service is recovered
- incident is resolved
- operator action is safe

This invariant is already enforced for `nq.witness.v1` by `WitnessPacket::validate`, which rejects observations carrying a top-level `claim` or `supports` key. The cut-over must preserve that enforcement on the Track A path.

### 2. Findings are not custody roots

A finding may be produced *from* witness material, but it is not the root observation artifact for claim custody.

If Track A must temporarily project witness packets from legacy finding state during cut-over, the resulting packet must say so explicitly (see "Transitional projection rule" below). A receipt that names a projected packet's digest is anchored to the projection, not to native observation.

### 3. `observed_at` is substrate time

`observed_at` means *when the substrate condition was observed*. Period.

It must not be silently replaced by:

- finding creation time
- import time
- evaluation time
- receipt time
- current wall-clock time

A projected witness packet whose `observed_at` cannot be traced back to substrate observation is broken. If the projection cannot recover a true `observed_at`, either:

- the packet must declare the limitation in its `projection_limits` and the evaluator must refuse to admit it as fresh testimony, or
- the projection must not be produced.

Faking `observed_at` from `generated_at` is the single most attractive shortcut here. It is also the cleanest way to launder the cut-over into incoherence.

### 4. `generated_at` is artifact time

`generated_at` means *when this packet was produced*. It may be later than `observed_at` (a projection always will be). **It does not refresh the observation.**

Freshness policy (`freshness_horizon`, per Slice 1c) is anchored on `observed_at`, never on `generated_at`. The helper `nq_core::preflight::freshness_horizon_from` already enforces this; the cut-over must not introduce a code path that anchors freshness on `generated_at`.

### 5. `cannot_testify` is first-class

Every Track A witness packet must declare what it cannot support. A disk witness can support `disk_state`. It cannot support `service_recovered`. A liveness witness can support some forms of `service_state`. It cannot support coverage unless the witness profile actually observes coverage.

`cannot_testify` ships on every preflight result today (per `crates/nq-core/src/preflight.rs`'s constitutional refusal lists). The witness packet itself should carry the *packet's* refusal surface — the conclusions this specific observation does not license — separately from the *claim kind's* refusal surface, which is the evaluator's responsibility.

## Transitional projection rule

This is the deadbolt. Otherwise six months from now you'll be staring at a receipt and asking whether it came from the new custody model or from a finding wearing novelty glasses.

During cut-over, `disk_state` (and later `ingest_state`, `dns_state`) may accept a *compatibility witness packet* projected from legacy detector / finding material. Such a packet must carry, in addition to the standard `nq.witness.v1` envelope:

- `custody_basis: "legacy_projection"` (the discriminator field; absent or `"native_observation"` on regular packets)
- A reference back to the source finding, e.g. `source_finding_id` or `source_finding_key`
- The original detector observation fields it could recover
- An explicit `projection_limits` list naming what the projection could not preserve
- An expanded `cannot_testify` list including `"native_witness_custody"` (a projected packet cannot anchor native custody by construction)

**Projected packets must not be indistinguishable from native packets on the wire.** A consumer that reads a receipt should be able to see, by inspecting the witness packet's `custody_basis`, whether the receipt's claim-custody chain bottoms out in real observation or in transitional compatibility furniture.

This affects receipts too: `Receipt` already carries `WitnessRef.digest`, but the *kind* of custody behind that digest is currently invisible to receipt consumers. Two options for surfacing it:

1. **Receipt-side derivation.** A future verb (e.g. `nq-monitor receipt check --packets` already-supplied case) reads the packets and reports their `custody_basis`. Custody kind never appears on the receipt itself.
2. **Receipt-side anchoring.** A field on `WitnessRef` (e.g. `custody_basis: "native_observation" | "legacy_projection"`) records what the packet was at receipt-emit time. Future replays see the basis without needing the packet.

Option 1 keeps the receipt envelope unchanged. Option 2 makes basis legible from the receipt alone. The design preflight does not pick — see open question 5 below.

## Wire shape sketch (additive to `nq.witness.v1`)

The existing `nq.witness.v1` envelope:

```rust
pub struct WitnessPacket {
    pub schema: String,
    pub witness_type: String,
    pub subject: String,
    pub access_path: String,
    pub observed_at: String,
    pub generated_at: String,
    pub observations: Vec<serde_json::Value>,
    pub coverage_limits: Vec<String>,
    pub dependencies: Vec<String>,
}
```

The minimum V1 cut-over extension (additive, optional, `skip_serializing_if = None`):

- `custody_basis: Option<String>` — `"native_observation"` or `"legacy_projection"`. Absent = the packet predates the cut-over.
- `source_finding_ref: Option<String>` — only when `custody_basis == "legacy_projection"`. Local reference (finding_key) to the source.
- `projection_limits: Vec<String>` — only when projected. Plain-prose enumeration of what the projection could not preserve.

The user's design discussions floated a wider shape (structured `subject`, `witness_profile` block, structured `coverage` with dimensions, structured `producer`, `claim_kind` on the packet). All of that is interesting and may eventually be right. None of it is in scope for the V1 cut-over.

Wider envelope = wider blast radius. The V1 cut-over should add custody without widening claim semantics. The wider envelope is a separate ratified change — likely the same forcing case that triggers `CLAIM_PREFLIGHT_REGISTRY_SHAPE_GAP`.

### Native example (V1 shape)

```json
{
  "schema": "nq.witness.v1",
  "witness_type": "fs_usage",
  "subject": "host:storage01/mount:/var",
  "access_path": "local_statvfs",
  "observed_at": "2026-05-24T20:13:22Z",
  "generated_at": "2026-05-24T20:13:24Z",
  "observations": [
    {
      "type": "fs_usage_state",
      "mount": "/var",
      "bytes_used_ratio": 0.91,
      "inodes_used_ratio": 0.72
    }
  ],
  "coverage_limits": [
    "single vantage; one filesystem"
  ],
  "dependencies": [],
  "custody_basis": "native_observation"
}
```

### Projection example (V1 shape)

```json
{
  "schema": "nq.witness.v1",
  "witness_type": "disk_state_legacy_projection",
  "subject": "host:storage01/pool:tank",
  "access_path": "legacy_finding_projection",
  "observed_at": "2026-05-24T20:13:22Z",
  "generated_at": "2026-05-24T20:15:02Z",
  "observations": [
    {
      "type": "zfs_pool_state_projected",
      "pool": "tank",
      "state": "degraded"
    }
  ],
  "coverage_limits": [
    "packet reconstructed from legacy finding state",
    "native witness packet unavailable",
    "custody root is transitional"
  ],
  "dependencies": [],
  "custody_basis": "legacy_projection",
  "source_finding_ref": "finding:zfs_pool_degraded:host:storage01:pool:tank",
  "projection_limits": [
    "original detector run metadata not preserved",
    "transport / encoding lineage unknown"
  ]
}
```

## Acceptance tests (pre-implementation)

These are the tests a Slice 2 implementation must pass before it ships. Filing them here so the implementation can't quietly drift past them.

### 1. Native fs witness supports `disk_state`

Given a native `fs_usage` witness packet, when `disk_state` preflight runs, then:

- The receipt's `WitnessRef.digest` is populated with the packet's digest.
- The receipt's verdict semantics match the existing Track A.0 behavior for the equivalent finding state (no public verdict-set change).
- The evaluator does not consult finding-derived coverage on this path.

### 2. Legacy projection is visibly marked

Given legacy detector / finding state, when a projected witness packet is produced, then:

- `custody_basis == "legacy_projection"`.
- The receipt's `WitnessRef.digest` is the projected packet's digest.
- The packet explicitly cannot testify to native witness custody.
- A consumer reading packet + receipt together can distinguish projection from native.

### 3. Finding cannot self-authorize

Given only a finding with no recoverable substrate-time `observed_at`, when `disk_state` preflight runs:

- Native witness packet path: preflight refuses native custody for this subject (a coverage entry reports `standing: "absent"` for the native witness; the finding does not get promoted into a packet).
- Or: a projected packet is produced with `projection_limits` naming the missing `observed_at`, and freshness logic treats it as unverifiable freshness.

The forbidden path: a packet whose `observed_at` was fabricated from `generated_at` or finding creation time, indistinguishable from a packet with substrate-time `observed_at`.

### 4. `generated_at` does not refresh `observed_at`

Given a stale observation projected into a new packet, when preflight evaluates freshness, then `freshness_horizon` is computed from `observed_at`, not from `generated_at`. (`freshness_horizon_from` in `nq-core::preflight` is already correct; the test enforces that the Track A cut-over does not introduce a parallel code path that gets this wrong.)

### 5. Liveness does not testify for coverage

Given green liveness material, when evaluating `disk_state` coverage, then coverage is not satisfied and the evaluator's `cannot_testify` (or the witness packet's `cannot_testify` for finer scope) explains the refusal. The keeper from `CLAIM_PREFLIGHT_EXISTING_WITNESSES.md` (*"green liveness is not permitted to testify for coverage"*) holds on the new path.

### 6. Slice 1d / 1e behavior on Track A receipts after cut-over

- `nq-monitor receipt check` works on Track A receipts (worked before, still works).
- `nq-monitor receipt replay` on a *native-custody* Track A receipt no longer returns `REPLAY_NOT_APPLICABLE` — it dispatches to a Track A replay path that re-runs the evaluator over supplied native packets, comparable to Track B.
- `nq-monitor receipt replay` on a *legacy-projection* Track A receipt may still return a bounded status (e.g. `REPLAY_NOT_APPLICABLE` with a different detail, or a new `REPLAY_PROJECTION_BASIS` status) — design open question 4.

## Open design questions

### Q1. Is the V1 cut-over additive on `nq.witness.v1`, or does it bump the schema?

Recommended: additive. New optional fields (`custody_basis`, `source_finding_ref`, `projection_limits`) with `skip_serializing_if = None`. Existing consumers see no shape change. Schema bump (`nq.witness.v2`) is reserved for a future ratified change that may also bring structured `subject`, `witness_profile`, etc.

### Q2. Where does `custody_basis` propagate?

On `WitnessPacket` definitively (it describes the packet). On `WitnessRef` in the receipt: open. See "Transitional projection rule" above for the two options. Recommendation: defer to a follow-up commit after the packet-side shape ships and gets exercised. Don't widen the receipt envelope speculatively.

### Q3. How does the projected-packet producer get the original `observed_at`?

Disk-state findings store `observed_at` on their underlying detector rows (`zfs_pool_state.observed_at`, SMART tables, etc.). The projection layer must read substrate-time, not finding-creation-time. If the substrate row is absent, the projection must refuse (option A) or produce a packet whose `observed_at` is missing / explicitly limited (option B). Recommendation: refuse rather than emit a structurally-broken packet.

### Q4. Does Slice 1e get a new replay status for projected receipts?

Today: `REPLAY_NOT_APPLICABLE` for all Track A. After cut-over:

- Native-custody Track A receipts → full replay support (new code path in `receipt_replay`).
- Projected-custody Track A receipts → ?

Options: keep them as `REPLAY_NOT_APPLICABLE` (with refined detail), or add a new status (`REPLAY_PROJECTION_BASIS`) that says "this receipt's witness custody is transitional; semantic replay is bounded." Recommendation: keep the bound status `REPLAY_NOT_APPLICABLE` with a refined detail string for V1. New replay statuses are a wire-shape commitment; only mint one if there's a forcing operator-side need.

### Q5. Does this force `CLAIM_PREFLIGHT_REGISTRY_SHAPE`?

Possibly. The cut-over introduces a Track A claim kind (`disk_state`) that now consumes witness packets like Track B does. That makes it the second registered evaluator family with packet-based custody. The third (`ingest_state` or `dns_state`, depending on which lands next) is the explicit forcing-case threshold per `CLAIM_PREFLIGHT_REGISTRY_SHAPE_GAP.md`.

Recommendation: do not generalize the registry shape in this slice. Disk first. Registry generalization is its own ratified change with its own design preflight. *The serpent can wait.*

### Q6. Does this affect the existing HTTP routes?

The HTTP preflight routes (`/api/preflight/disk-state/{host}` etc.) serve `PreflightResult` today. After cut-over, the evaluator's internal path changes (witness packets in, instead of finding state in) but the result shape should not. Public wire = unchanged.

If the cut-over surfaces a need to expose witness packets via HTTP (e.g. so a consumer can supply them to `nq-monitor receipt replay`), that's a separate route surface. Out of scope for V1.

## What the V1 cut-over does *not* do

Bounded explicitly so the slice does not eat the project:

- **Does not widen the public verdict set.** Same eight verdicts, same `cannot_testify` lists, same per-kind contract versions.
- **Does not change the HTTP preflight routes' response shape.**
- **Does not generalize the registry.** `CLAIM_PREFLIGHT_REGISTRY_SHAPE` is deferred.
- **Does not affect Track B.** Track B already consumes witness packets; this slice is only Track A.
- **Does not retire the existing detector machinery.** Detectors continue to populate finding tables; the projection layer reads from those tables. Detectors' contract is unaffected.
- **Does not introduce a new schema version.** Additive fields on `nq.witness.v1`.
- **Does not require Slice 3 (`service_recovered`).** Independent slice.
- **Does not commit to receipt-bundle, packet-retention, or cross-host-attestation work.** Those are forward pressure from `CLAIM_CUSTODY.md`; not in scope here.

## Slice 2 rule (compressed)

```text
Track A may consume witness packets.
Track A may temporarily project witness packets from legacy material.
Track A may not pretend projected custody is native custody.
Track A may not allow findings to become the witnesses that authorize their own conclusions.
```

That's the slice. Everything above is the discipline required to ship it without quietly rebuilding the old shape.
